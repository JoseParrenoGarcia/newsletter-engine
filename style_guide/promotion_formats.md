# Promotion Formats

Jose's templates for LinkedIn and Substack promotional posts. Both platforms use the same format. `/promote` skill reads this file as ground truth.

---

## Launch Post Template

Used on day X — when the post goes live.

```
🗞️ New post is live! [Title]

[Bold claim — the core argument of the article, 1 sentence. Provocative if earned by the content.]
[Optional: consequence or framing line — 1 short sentence]

What's inside:
🔹 [Key insight 1] — 1 line
🔹 [Key insight 2] — 1 line
🔹 [Key insight 3] — 1 line

[Optional credibility/framing line — e.g. "I built this across 6 months of real projects."]

💬 [Engagement question — relatable + slightly painful]
👇 [Link]
```

**Guidelines:**
- The bold claim is the article's thesis restated as a standalone statement — not a summary of what the article contains
- 3 insight bullets should be the 3 most important takeaways — ideally one per major section
- Credibility line is optional; only include if it genuinely adds weight
- Engagement question should feel real, not rhetorical
- CTA is always the article link

## Final Notes Template (v2) — Section Deep-Dives

Used on day X+1, X+2, X+3 — one post per promotable section of the article.

```
[Strong opinionated opening — 1 sentence. Bold claim about the section's core idea. Slightly provocative. No setup, no context.]
[Optional reinforcement — 1 short line: consequence, warning, or framing. No references to "this section" or "previous post".]

Let's break it down:
🔹 [Core insight] → [Direct, punchy explanation — 1–2 lines max]
👉 [Why it matters — not a definition]
🔹 [Core insight] → [Practical or behavioural explanation]
👉 [How it works in reality when possible]
🔹 [Core insight] → [System-level takeaway]
👉 [Scalability / maintainability / predictability angle]

[Optional credibility / punchline — e.g. "This is where most teams go wrong..." or "If you don't believe me..."]

💬 [Engagement question — relatable + slightly painful]
👇 [CTA — link to post, share experience, or comment prompt]
```

**Guidelines:**
- Opening must stand alone — a reader who hasn't seen the article should be able to engage with the claim on its own
- 3 bullet points are non-negotiable — the template only works with exactly 3
- Each 🔹 bullet has a paired 👉 line — never one without the other
- The 👉 lines escalate: why it matters → how it works → system-level implication
- No references to "in my blog post", "as I wrote", or "in section X" — promote the idea, not the article
- CTA goes at the very end, after the engagement question
